Why are snaps good for C/C++ projects?

  • Snaps are easy to discover and install. Millions of users can browse and install snaps graphically in the Snap Store or from the command-line.
  • Snaps install and run the same across Linux. They bundle the exact versions of your app’s dependencies.
  • Snaps automatically update to the latest version. Four times a day, users’ systems will check for new versions and upgrade in the background.
  • Upgrades are not disruptive. Because upgrades are not in-place, users can keep your app open as it’s upgraded in the background.
  • Upgrades are safe. If your app fails to upgrade, users automatically roll back to the previous revision.

Here’s how dosbox uses it:

name: dosbox
version: "0.74-svn"
summary: DOS emulator
description: |
  DOSBox is a x86 emulator with Tandy/Hercules/CGA/EGA/VGA/SVGA graphics
  sound and DOS. It's been designed to run old DOS games under platforms that
  don't support it.

confinement: devmode
base: core18

parts:
  dosbox:
    plugin: autotools
    source-type: tar
    source: http://source.dosbox.com/dosboxsvn.tgz
    build-packages:
      - g++
      - make
      - libsdl1.2-dev
      - libpng12-dev
      - libsdl-net1.2-dev
      - libsdl-sound1.2-dev
      - libasound2-dev

apps:
  dosbox:
    command: dosbox
